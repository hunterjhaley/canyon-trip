First things first

When to go

Locked to Thanksgiving week so nobody misses a day of school. Leave Friday afternoon right after early release, run the loop, and roll home the following Sunday. Nine nights, built around the official Livingston ISD 2026-27 calendar.

🥇

Thanksgiving: Fri Nov 20 to Sun Nov 29, 2026

Zero school missed9 nights outThe quietest the park ever gets

This is the plan. LISD is closed the full Thanksgiving week (Nov 23 to 27), and Friday Nov 20 is an early-release day, so we pull out Friday afternoon the moment the kids are sprung and roll home Sunday the 29th without anyone missing a single class.

The trade, named plainly: the rim sits at 7,000 feet, so late November runs crisp 40s and 50s by day, below freezing at night in the coaches, and a real chance of an early snow or ice event. Build in a weather flex day and it is a gorgeous, empty, low-light version of the canyon. Thanksgiving Day itself, most kitchens close, so we plan a big meal in the coaches.

Other windows we weighed

For the record, so everyone sees the thinking behind Thanksgiving:

  • Fall break, early OctoberThe best weather of the year, rim days in the 60s and 70s, but it costs two days of missed school. Thanksgiving trades a little cold for zero absences.
  • Spring break, mid MarchAlso zero school missed and warmer nights, with Hill Country bluebonnets on the way home. A strong backup if the family would rather not gamble on November weather.
  • Christmas breakThe biggest window at 16 days and no school missed, but the riskiest time to move two 45 foot coaches through snow and hard freezes. Off the table unless we build in real weather cushion.
Off the table: the February break (deepest winter at the rim) and April to May (school in session, STAAR testing). Summer works in a pinch but means peak crowds and monsoon season.

Before we roll

Big rig ground rules

  • Every stop below fits 40 to 45 foot coaches.All are rated 4.2 stars or better with big rig reviews checked. Pull-through or escorted parking at nearly every one.
  • Book the canyon nights first, and soon.Trailer Village and holiday weekends fill fast, and Thanksgiving is a peak week at the canyon, so these get locked first. Lock the canyon dates the week we pick a window, then build everything else around them.
  • No tow car, on purpose.The coaches, the canyon train, free shuttles, and two UberXLs in the cities move all nine of us. Every day below carries a teal Getting Around bar showing exactly how.
  • Diesel budget.Figure roughly 350 gallons per coach round trip at 7 to 8 mpg. Ballpark $1,200 to $1,400 per coach at today's prices.
  • Drive days are capped near 5 hourseverywhere we could manage it, with the two exceptions flagged in red. Wheels up by 9, parked by 3, pool by 4.
No tow car needed

How we get around

  • The coaches handle every highway stop.Cadillac Ranch, the pie roadhouse, The Thing?, the caverns, even White Sands: all built as pull-in stops with big rig parking. No second vehicle needed.
  • Two UberXLs move all nine of us in the cities.Grapevine, Santa Fe, Tucson, and Las Cruces all have solid coverage. Batch the outings: ride in once, spend the day, ride back once.
  • Smaller markets: schedule, don't hail.In Santa Fe and Fredericksburg, book rides the night before with Uber Reserve so two XLs show up on time instead of hoping.
  • The canyon and Sedona run on trains and shuttles.Free park shuttles and the Williams train cover the whole rim, and Sedona runs free trailhead buses plus a $2 on-demand town shuttle. The two days that looked hardest are actually the easiest.
Watch the teal bars: every day below has a Getting Around line. The handful of spots we re-worked so the trip needs no tow car are called out right where they happen, and nobody loses an experience.

Option A: Williams + the Grand Canyon Railway

Grand Canyon Railway RV Park • Williams, AZ
★ 4.6 (1,230)Walk to the train depotIndoor pool accessPet hotel on site

Park the rigs and never think about canyon parking again. A historic train runs from the depot two blocks away straight to the rim: wild west shootout show before boarding, singing cowboys on board, and outlaws on horseback "rob" the train on the ride home. The 4, 6, and 12 year old will talk about it for years, and the 66 year old rides in comfort. Williams itself is a Route 66 postcard town with diners and shops.

📋 Big rig notes

Long level pull-throughs, easy in and out off I-40. Sits next to the BNSF line, so expect an occasional train horn. Hotel pool access is included but has been under renovation recently, so confirm it is open when booking.

Hilltop RV Park

Fort Stockton, TX • right off I-10
★ 4.6 (681)Pool + walking trailWide pull-throughs

Morning at the dunes first: coaches up US-70, oversized parking on Dunes Drive, two hours of sledding while the sand is cool, then back through Las Cruces and east into West Texas. Twelve-year full-timers call Hilltop one of the cleanest parks they have seen: flags from every state, metal art everywhere, fire pits, and a pool to burn off the last of the kids' energy.
